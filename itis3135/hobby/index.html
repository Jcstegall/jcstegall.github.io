When To Go

Fall is magical in the Appalachians, with vibrant foliage painting the trails. For cooler, quieter hikes, consider early spring or late summer. Be prepared for sudden weather changes at higher elevations.

How to

What to bring:

  • Hiking boots- A must for good traction and preventing injuries
  • Backpack- A comfotable and lightweight backpack makes all the difference
  • Water and Food: Better to overpack than underpack as dehydration and malnutrition can lead to worse consenquences down the road
  • First Aid: Always be prepared for injuries that can occur on the trails
  • Trail Map: Many trails do not have service so a physical map or a downloaded version will prevent confusion and keep you on trails

Safety Tips

  • Watch our for wildlife: Research and become knowledgeable about wildlife and best practices. Black bears are around, make noise while hiking and dont leave food out.
  • Stay on trail: Getting lost is easier than you might think, stick to marked trails and download your map on your phone will help!
  • Check the Weather: Weather can change fast, hiking in thunderstorms is a big risk.
  • Let someone know your plan: Tell a friend or family member your hiking plans and estimated return time
