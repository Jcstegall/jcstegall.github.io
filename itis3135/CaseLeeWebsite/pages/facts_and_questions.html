Everything you need to know about Paintless Dent Repair

About Paintless Dent Repair

Paintless Dent Repair (PDR) is a specialized technique for removing minor dents from a vehicle's body without the need for painting. It's a cost-effective and efficient alternative to traditional body shop repairs for many types of damage.

Eco-Friendly

PDR doesn't use chemicals, fillers, or paints, making it an environmentally friendly repair option.

Time-Saving

Most PDR repairs can be completed in a few hours, rather than days required for traditional body shop repairs.

Preserves Value

PDR maintains your vehicle's original factory finish, which helps preserve its resale value.

Cost-Effective

PDR typically costs 25-50% less than traditional body shop repairs for qualifying dents.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about Paintless Dent Repair? Find answers to common questions below.

What types of dents can be repaired with PDR?

PDR works best on minor to medium-sized dents where the paint hasn't been damaged. It's ideal for hail damage, door dings, minor creases, and dents from shopping carts or balls. The success of PDR depends on the dent's location, size, and depth. Generally, if a dent is smaller than a dinner plate and hasn't stretched the metal too severely, PDR can likely fix it.

How long does a PDR repair take?

The time required for PDR depends on the dent's severity and location. Simple door dings might take 30-60 minutes, while more complex dents or multiple dents (like hail damage) could take several hours. Most repairs can be completed the same day, which is much faster than traditional body shop repairs that often take days or weeks.

How much does PDR cost?

PDR costs vary based on the damage severity, location, and vehicle make/model. Simple door dings typically range from $75-$200, while larger dents may cost $200-$500. Hail damage can range from $1,000-$5,000 depending on severity, but is often covered by insurance. PDR generally costs 25-50% less than traditional repair methods.

Will PDR affect my car's paint or finish?

No, PDR doesn't affect your vehicle's paint or finish. It preserves the original factory paint, which helps maintain your vehicle's value. There's no risk of paint mismatching, overspray, or other issues that can occur with traditional body repair. This is one of the biggest advantages of PDR.

Does insurance cover PDR?

Yes, most insurance policies cover PDR repairs, especially for hail damage or if another person caused the damage. PDR is generally preferred by insurance companies because it costs less than traditional repairs. Depending on your deductible, you may have little to no out-of-pocket expense. We can help you work with your insurance company to file a claim.

What is the PDR process like?

The PDR process involves several steps:

  1. Assessment: The technician examines the dent to determine if PDR is suitable.
  2. Access: The technician gains access to the backside of the dent, often by removing interior panels or trimming.
  3. Visualization: Special lighting helps the technician see the dent from all angles.
  4. Repair: Using specialized tools, the technician carefully massages the metal back into its original position from behind.
  5. Quality check: The repair is inspected to ensure it meets professional standards.
Can PDR repair hail damage?

Yes, PDR is the preferred method for repairing hail damage. It's particularly effective for the multiple small dents that hailstorms typically cause. PDR can restore your vehicle to its pre-storm condition without affecting the original paint. For extensive hail damage, the process may take longer, but it's still usually faster and less expensive than traditional body shop repairs.

What dents CANNOT be repaired with PDR?

PDR has some limitations. It generally cannot repair:

  • Dents with damaged paint or scratches
  • Dents on body panels with restricted access
  • Dents with very sharp creases or folds in the metal
  • Dents where the metal has been stretched too far
  • Damage to plastic panels (which don't respond the same way as metal)
  • Damage from major collisions

We can assess your vehicle and let you know if PDR is a suitable repair method for your specific damage.
